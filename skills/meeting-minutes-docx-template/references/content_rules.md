# Content Rules

## Summary

- Lead with conclusions.
- Prefer concrete variables: orders, capacity, revenue, profit, customers, products, costs, timing, risks.
- Keep each topic coherent. Use 2-4 detail points per topic when possible.
- Do not force a detail into a growth-curve label unless the source notes clearly support that mapping.

## Company Overview

- Write objectively.
- Introduce business scope, products, strategic direction, and relevant investments.
- Do not turn the overview into an investment recommendation.
- Use 2-3 paragraphs unless source material is very short.

## Q&A

- You may create natural questions from raw notes.
- Answers must be grounded in source material.
- Do not invent missing numbers, contract status, customer wins, margins, or dates.
- If a point is inferred from context, use conservative wording: `从材料看`, `更适合表述为`, `有望`, `预计`, `尚未明确`, `仍需跟踪`.
- If the source notes contain an ambiguous label, preserve the source mapping or explicitly state the uncertainty.

## Common Pitfall

If raw notes say:

```text
第二成长曲线
数据中心：目前还没有合同...
```

Then do not say another business is the second growth curve unless the notes clearly say so. Put the other business under `新能源转型方向`, `新增量来源`, or another conservative label.
